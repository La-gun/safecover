Terms & Conditions – Insurance Policy

Important: Please read these terms and conditions carefully. They set out the rights and responsibilities of both the insurer and the insured (policyholder). By purchasing this insurance, you confirm that you have read, understood and agree to be bound by these terms.

1. Definitions

Insurer means the insurance company providing the cover (as stated in your policy schedule). Insured / Policyholder / You means the person(s) named on the policy. We / Us means the insurer. Policy means the contract of insurance between you and us. Premium means the amount you pay for the insurance. Sum insured means the maximum amount we will pay under the policy. Excess means the amount you must pay towards each claim before we pay the remainder.

2. Type of Cover

This policy provides cover as described in your Insurance Product Information Document (IPID) and policy schedule. Cover types include:

  • Goods-in-Transit: Loss, damage or theft of insured goods during transit from dispatch to delivery.
  • Jewellery & Watch (All Risks): Accidental damage, theft (with police report), mechanical breakdown (watches), worldwide cover.
  • Other product types as specified in your policy schedule.

3. Duty of Fair Presentation (Insurance Act 2015)

Mandatory – Your responsibility: Before the contract is entered into, you must make a fair presentation of the risk. This means you must:
  • Disclose every material circumstance which you know or ought to know, or give us sufficient information to put a prudent insurer on notice that it needs to make further enquiries.
  • Disclose material circumstances in a manner which would be reasonably clear and accessible to a prudent insurer.
  • Not make a misrepresentation. A misrepresentation is careless if made in breach of the duty of fair presentation.

Material circumstance means something that would influence the judgement of a prudent insurer in deciding whether to take the risk and on what terms. If you fail to make a fair presentation, we may have remedies under the Insurance Act 2015 (including avoidance of the contract, proportionately reduced payouts, or termination).

4. Eligibility

You must:

  • Be aged 18 or over.
  • Be resident in the United Kingdom (England, Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland).
  • Provide accurate information about the insured items and your circumstances.

5. What is Covered

Cover is as set out in your IPID and policy schedule. In summary:

  • Goods-in-Transit: Loss, damage or theft of goods during the specified transit period.
  • Jewellery: Accidental damage; theft (with police report); loss proven as theft; mechanical breakdown (watches); worldwide cover.

Cover is subject to the sum insured, excess, and exclusions below.

6. What is Not Covered (Exclusions)

We will not pay for:

  • Loss not proved as theft: Unexplained disappearance, misplacement or loss where theft cannot be evidenced with a police report.
  • Wear and tear: Gradual deterioration, scratching or damage from normal use.
  • Unattended items: Theft from an unattended vehicle or from a location where the item was left unattended (e.g. gym locker, public changing room).
  • Intentional acts: Damage or loss caused deliberately by you or a member of your household.
  • War, terrorism, confiscation: Loss or damage from war, terrorism, civil unrest, or confiscation by authorities.
  • Warranty/servicing: Damage covered by manufacturer warranty or due to routine servicing.
  • Goods-in-Transit: Wear and tear; inherent vice; confiscation; goods shipped in contravention of carrier terms.

7. Policy Limits and Excess

Our liability is limited to the sum insured stated in your policy schedule. You must pay the excess (deductible) for each claim before we pay the remainder. The excess is stated in your policy schedule (typically £50 for jewellery; may vary for other products).

8. Your Obligations

8.1 At the start of the contract

  • Provide accurate information about insured items (description, value, proof of purchase/valuation).
  • Declare any previous claims, losses or refusals of insurance.
  • Store items securely when not in use (e.g. safe, locked cabinet for jewellery).

8.2 During the contract

  • Notify us of any change in circumstances that may affect the risk (e.g. change of address, new high-value item).
  • Keep items in secure storage when not worn; do not leave unattended in vehicles or public places.
  • Maintain proof of purchase and valuation documents.

8.3 When making a claim

  • Report loss/theft within 24 hours to the police and to us.
  • Obtain a police report and crime reference number for theft or attempted theft.
  • Provide: proof of purchase, valuation certificate, photographs, and any other evidence we request.
  • Cooperate fully with our claims investigation.

9. Claims Procedure

To make a claim:

  1. Contact us as soon as possible (within 24 hours for theft/loss).
  2. For theft: report to the police and obtain a crime reference number.
  3. Complete our claims form and provide all requested documents (proof of purchase, valuation, police report, photographs).
  4. We will assess your claim and may request further information. We aim to respond within 15 working days of receiving a complete claim.

Claims are subject to verification and approval. We may appoint loss adjusters or investigators. Fraudulent claims will be reported to the police and may result in prosecution.

10. Premium and Payment

You must pay the premium in full (or as agreed for instalment plans) before cover begins. If you pay by instalments, interest may apply. Failure to pay may result in cancellation of the policy.

11. Policy Period

Cover begins and ends as stated in your policy schedule. For goods-in-transit, cover is from dispatch to delivery. For jewellery and other annual policies, cover runs for the period stated (e.g. 12 months from the effective date).

12. Cooling-Off and Cancellation

14-day cooling-off period: You may cancel this policy within 14 days of the start of cover (or of receiving the policy documents, if later) and receive a full refund of the premium, provided you have not made a claim. To cancel, contact us in writing or by the contact method stated in your policy documents.

After the cooling-off period: You may cancel at any time. We will refund any premium for the unexpired period, minus an administration fee (if applicable and as stated in your policy). We may cancel the policy for non-payment, fraud, or material non-disclosure, subject to giving you notice where required by law.

13. Complaints

If you are not satisfied with our service or a decision we have made, please contact us. We will acknowledge your complaint promptly and aim to resolve it within 8 weeks. If we cannot resolve your complaint to your satisfaction, you may refer it to the Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS):

The Financial Ombudsman Service is free to use. Their decision may be binding on us but not necessarily on you.

14. Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS)

If we are unable to meet our obligations, you may be entitled to compensation from the Financial Services Compensation Scheme. This depends on the type of business and the circumstances of the claim. Further information is available from the FSCS: www.fscs.org.uk or 0800 678 1100.

You have rights including access, rectification, erasure, and to object to processing. For more information, see our Privacy Notice or contact our Data Protection Officer.
